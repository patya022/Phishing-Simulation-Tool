How to Identify Phishing Emails:

  • Check the Sender: Look carefully at the sender's email address. Phishing emails often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but are slightly altered.
  • Suspicious Links: Hover over any links without clicking on them to see where they lead. Phishing emails often direct you to fake websites.
  • Grammar and Spelling: Many phishing emails contain unusual grammar or spelling errors.
  • Urgency: Phishing emails often try to create a sense of urgency or fear, asking you to act immediately.

What to Do Next:

If you think you've encountered a phishing email, do not click on any links or provide your credentials. Report it to your IT department or the email provider.
